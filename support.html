What is CD

 CD or compressed disc (English: Compact Disc) is one of the types of optical discs, they are usually made of plastic, 4.75 inches in diameter, using optical recording method to store about 80 minutes. audio or 700 MB of digitally encrypted computer data.


CD-R(Compact Disk – Recordable) :

 CD-R is a blank CD in which data can be stored once. After storing the data, it is converted to a CD-ROM. It is made of a reflective metal disc with a layer of green or opaque dye on top. It is cheaper than CD-RW and CD-ROM. In CD-R data cannot be overwritten. The advantage of CD-R is that any version of CD player whether old or newer can read CD-R easily.


CD-RW (Compact Disk – ReWritable) :

 CD-RW is an erasable and reusable CD. It is made of a reflective metal disc with a special metal layer on top. It is the cheapest CD of all. It is more expensive than both CD-R and CD-ROM. The disadvantage with CD-RW is that most older versions of CD players cannot read it. In some properties, it also shares some similarities with CD-RW.
